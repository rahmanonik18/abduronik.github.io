Research Focus

My research centers on security and privacy in mobile applications, investigating how apps collect, store, and share user data, often contradicting their stated privacy policies. I develop forensic methods to extract and analyze app data from device storage and network traffic, enabling deeper understanding of actual data handling practices.

I am designing a novel framework using Large Language Models (LLMs) to automatically parse privacy policies and compare them with empirically observed data collection behaviors. As part of this work, I'm developing the App Privacy Compliance Index (APCI), a standardized metric to quantify app transparency and privacy alignment.

My broader goal is to build tools and frameworks that empower end-users, investigators, and regulators to audit mobile applications effectively, bridging the gap between privacy claims and real-world behaviors.
